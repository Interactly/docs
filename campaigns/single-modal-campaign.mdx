# About

Single-modal campaigns provide a streamlined, voice-only communication channel for automated phone calls. These campaigns focus exclusively on direct voice interactions with your AI assistant, making them perfect for appointment reminders, quick surveys, confirmations, and simple outreach scenarios.

<Info>
  Single-modal campaigns are ideal when you only need voice interactions. For campaigns requiring SMS notifications or follow-up messages, explore [Multi-Modal Campaigns](/campaigns/multi-modal-campaign).
</Info>

## What Makes It Single-Modal?

Single-modal campaigns are designed for **voice interactions only**:

<Card title="Voice-Only Features" icon="phone">
  - ‚úÖ Direct phone calls to contacts
  - ‚úÖ AI assistant-powered conversations
  - ‚úÖ Real-time call processing
  - ‚úÖ Call outcome tracking
  - ‚ùå No pre-call SMS notifications
  - ‚ùå No post-call messaging
  - ‚ùå No text-based interactions
</Card>

#### How Campaign Timing Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Schedule Multiple Dates">
    Add as many campaign dates as needed, each with custom timing windows

    <Card title="Example Schedule" icon="calendar">
      - **Day 1**: January 15, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
      - **Day 2**: January 16, 2:00 PM - 8:00 PM
      - **Day 3**: January 20, 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM
    </Card>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Automatic Progression">
    Campaign moves through scheduled dates in sequence, making calls during each time window
  </Step>

  <Step title="Smart Completion">
    Campaign ends when **either** condition is met:
    - ‚úÖ All calls reach their final stage (completed/failed)
    - ‚úÖ All scheduled dates and times have passed
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="Campaign Completion Logic">
  **Important**: If your campaign completes all calls before reaching later scheduled dates, those remaining dates will be skipped automatically.

  **Example**: If you schedule 3 days but all contacts are successfully called on day 1, days 2 and 3 won't execute.

  This ensures efficient resource usage and prevents unnecessary processing.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Use multiple dates with different timings to accommodate varying contact preferences, time zones, or business operating hours.
</Tip>

## Campaign Status Tracking

### Call Status Progression

<Steps>
  <Step title="Received">
    Initial status when campaign launches and contacts are loaded
  </Step>

  <Step title="Validation">
    - **Ready**: Contact validation successful, ready to call
    - **Failed**: Validation failed (invalid number, missing data)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Queued">
    Calls are scheduled and waiting for their turn based on campaign timing
  </Step>

  <Step title="Processing">
    - **Ringing**: Phone is ringing, waiting for answer
    - **Processing**: Active conversation in progress
    - **End**: Call completed successfully
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Campaign Status Overview

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Processing" icon="gear">
    Campaign is actively making calls and managing conversations
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Completed" icon="check-circle">

  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

#### **Delete Campaign**:

- Click on ‚Äúdelete campaign‚Äù , to delete the campaign.
- The campaign along with the calls will be deleted.
- Once a campaign is deleted , it can‚Äôt be restored.

#### **Pause the campaign**:

- If you want to pause the campaign while the campaign is running, you can do that by clicking on the ‚Äúpause campaign‚Äù from the three dots.
- The calls that are already running will be continued and the calls that are in queued state will remain in queued until the campaign is resumed.

#### **Stop Campaign**:

- A campaign can be aborted by clicking on ‚Äústop campaign‚Äù .
- All the calls that are not triggered will be aborted.
- The campaign status will be changed to ‚Äúcompleted‚Äù once all the running calls are either ‚Äúfinished‚Äù or ‚Äúfailed‚Äù.

<Warning>
  **Action Behavior**:
  - **Pause**: Running calls continue, queued calls wait
  - **Stop**: Immediate halt - remaining calls are marked as aborted
  - **Actions are irreversible** once executed
</Warning>

- ‚úÖ Quick surveys or data collection calls

### Choose Multi-Modal Campaigns When:
- üì± You need pre-call SMS notifications

## API Reference - Campaign Actions

For programmatic control of your campaigns, use the Campaign Actions API:

<Card title="Campaign Action API" icon="play">
  **Endpoint**: [Action Campaign by ID](/api-reference/campaigns/campaigns/action-campaign-by-id)

  Control campaign execution through API calls:
  - **Launch**: Start your configured campaign
  - **Pause**: Temporarily halt call processing while maintaining queue
  - **Resume**: Continue paused campaigns from where they left off
  - **Stop**: Permanently abort remaining calls in the campaign
</Card>

<Info>
  This API provides the same campaign control actions available in the dashboard interface, allowing you to build automated campaign management workflows.
</Info>
